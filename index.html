NATURAL PRODUCT MINI PROJECT

Merasma Flakes

Project Purpose

This Natural Product Mini Project was undertaken by students of the Department of Pharmacognosy and Herbal Medicine, School of Pharmacy and Pharmaceutical Sciences, University of Cape Coast. The objective was to formulate and analyze a natural product (Marasma Flakes) derived from local raw materials using standard pharmacognostic procedures.

Website Purpose

This website documents the entire process — from raw material selection through processing, screening, and final packaging. It serves as a digital portfolio for the project, promoting transparency, educational value, and accessibility for academic or professional review.

Product Preparation

Step 1: Raw Material Selection

The raw materials — Cyperus esculentus (tiger nut), Cocos nucifera (coconut), Zingiber officinale (ginger) and honey — were carefully sourced from verified and trusted suppliers to ensure quality, authenticity, and safety for formulation.

Step 2: Processing of Raw Materials

The purchased raw materials were thoroughly washed with clean water to remove impurities. Ginger, coconut and tigernut were then grated using sterile equipment to obtain desirable size and shape, increase surface area and aid drying.

Step 3: Drying of Raw Materials

The grated materials were oven-dried at a controlled temperature of 75°C for a period of 24 hours. This ensured consistent moisture reduction while preserving key phytochemical constituents.

Step 4: Phytochemical Screening

After drying, a portion of the samples were blended into fine powder. Ethanol and water were used as solvents to extract the phytoconstituents, which were then subjected to phytochemical screening to identify the presence of compounds such as flavonoids, alkaloids, tannins, and saponins.

Step 5: Analytical Screening

Thin Layer Chromatography (TLC) was carried out on the ginger extract to verify and confirm the specific phytochemical constituents and to assess the quality of the material

Step 6: Glazing and Drying with Honey

The dried and powdered raw materials were weighed and mixed in a ratio of 3:2:1 for tiger nut, coconut, and ginger respectively. The resulting mixture was then glazed with natural honey to enhance flavor, texture, and shelf-life.

Step 7: Weighing and Packaging of Finished Product

The finished Marasma Flakes were weighed into appropriate portions and sealed in airtight containers to maintain freshness, stability, and hygiene until use or distribution.

About Product

This product, Marasma Flakes , is a functional food formulation composed of three key natural ingredients: Cyperus esculentus (tiger nut), Cocos nucifera (coconut), Zingiber officinale (ginger). Phytochemical screening revealed the presence of several bioactive constituents, each contributing therapeutic or health-promoting effects:

Indications

  • Tiger Nut: Contains flavonoids, alkaloids, and saponins. hese contribute to antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and digestive-supportive properties. It's also traditionally used to enhance libido and improve energy levels.
  • Coconut: showed the presence of tannins and saponins. Coconut is known for its antimicrobial, antifungal, and moisturizing properties. It also provides a rich source of healthy fats that support cardiovascular health..
  • Ginger: Contains flavonoids and essential oils. Ginger is widely recognized for its anti-nausea, anti-inflammatory, and gastroprotective effects. It also aids in digestion and improves circulation.

Potential Side Effects

  • Gastrointestinal discomfort (such as bloating or diarrhea) if taken in excess.
  • Possible heartburn or acid reflux due to ginger content.
  • Allergic reactions in people sensitive to coconut or nuts.
    • Users are advised to consume the product in moderate portions and observe how their body responds, especially on first use.

Contraindications

    Merasma Flakes should be avoided in any of the following cases:
  • Allergy to any component (tiger nut, coconut, ginger or honey).
  • Presence of gallstones or gallbladder disease (due to ginger).
  • Infants under 1 year (due to honey content which carries a risk of botulism in infants).
  • Pregnant women in the first trimester (without professional advice).

Drug–Drug Interactions

    Although this is a food-based product, some interactions may occur, especially due to the pharmacological activity of ginger:
  • Anticoagulants (e.g., warfarin, aspirin): Ginger may increase bleeding risk.
  • Antihypertensives: Ginger may mildly lower blood pressure, adding to medication effects.
  • Antidiabetic medications: May increase the risk of hypoglycemia due to ginger's glucose-lowering effects.
  • Patients on chronic medications should consult a healthcare provider before regular consumption.

Conclusion

Marasma Flakes is a promising functional food product combining the health benefits of tiger nut, coconut, and ginger. The formulation was developed using validated methods that ensured quality, safety, and efficacy. The natural phytoconstituents present offer a wide range of therapeutic properties, making the product suitable for both preventive and supportive wellness uses.
